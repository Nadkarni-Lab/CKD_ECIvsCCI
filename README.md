Background: CKD and dialysis are associated with increased mortality. Comorbidity indices like the CCI, ECI adn wECI have been validated in other conditions but not well studied in CKD and dialysis patients.
Here, we selected a group of patients from MSHS  with advanced CKD (CKD3/4), CKD 4 alone and those needing chronic dialysis and assessed the effect of each of these indices in improving prediction of 1-year mortality. 

Cohort inclusion/exclusion criteria
CKD3/4 by serum Cr (CKD4 calculated with CKD-EPI 2021)
    -Inclusion: Patients age > 18 CKD3/4 15 <= eGFR <- 60 (for at least 90 days before initial visit in OP or IP setting along with follow up visit within 1 year of inital visit.
    - Exclusion: kideny txp at baseline, dialysis at baseline, age < 18, no f/u 
CKD 4: 
    - Inclusion: Patients age > 18 CKD3/4 15 <= eGFR <- 60 (for at least 90 days before initial visit in OP or IP setting along with follow up visit within 1 year of inital visit.
    - Exclusion: kideny txp at baseline, dialysis at baseline, age < 18, no f/u 
Dialysis: by ICD 10 code
    - Inclusion: Patients age > 18 CKD3/4 15 <= eGFR <- 60 (for at least 90 days before initial visit in OP or IP setting along with follow up visit within 1 year of inital visit.
    - Exclusion: kideny txp at baseline, dialysis at baseline, age < 18, no f/u 
ECI, CCI and wECI added to baseline model which includes demographics + clinical patient level characteristics
    Split data 70:30 into training and testing cohorts and assessed efficacy of each model in predicting 1-year mortality

    AUROCs compared with DeLong test to estimate statistically significant improvements.
